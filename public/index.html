Relational databases like MySQL, PostgreSQL and SQLite3 represent and store data in tables and rows. They’re based on a branch of algebraic set theory known as relational algebra. Mongo query targets of data are technically represented as BSON (binary JASON). Relational databases use Structured Querying Language (SQL), making them a good choice for applications that involve the management of several transactions.
